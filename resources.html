Frequently Asked Questions

Simple answers to typical queries about lithium battery cells, grids, and recycling.

Modern lithium packs in Tesla and Ola vehicles are engineered for extreme durability. Standard packs will degrade less than 15-20% over **10 to 15 years** or **150,000 to 200,000 miles** of active driving. Manufacturers warranty battery packs for at least 8 years or 100,000 miles.

Curbside street chargers, office parking garages, public supermarket chargers, and rapid DC Supercharger networks provide seamless charging. Many EV drivers charge their vehicles weekly while shopping or working.

Yes. Under sub-freezing temperatures, chemical flow in lithium batteries slows and running cabin heating grids pulls electrical current. EVs can lose up to **20% to 30%** of ranges in freezing weather. Pre-conditioning the battery while plugged in minimizes this effect.

Absolutely. High-tech closed-loop recycling loops extract and recover up to **95% of active raw minerals** (cobalt, lithium, nickel, copper). These minerals are refined and reused to manufacture brand-new battery packs, creating a circular lifecycle.
